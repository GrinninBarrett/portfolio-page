A bit about me

A lifelong musician, my automatic choice of major in university was music, and later music education. I have taught music for 4 years, with a 4-year break in the middle to live in South Korea and teach ESL, including 3 years of kindergarten teaching. While music is something I plan to enjoy forever, I realized some time ago that teaching is not. Rather than continue to accept that being a music teacher is my only possible path in life, I decided to pursue another love of mine - technology.

I've always loved eyeing the newest gadgets, but have also developed a strange love for really old things, like typewriters and vintage keyboards. As someone who always loved tech, but was too deeply involved in the pursuit of a career in music, I long viewed the field of technology, in a broad sense, as something out of my reach - something not meant for a musical creative like me. I've realized, now, that the two seemingly opposite extremes of logical thinking and creativity are not mutually exclusive. In fact, it seems they're more intertwined than I ever imagined. I'm enjoying growing this new understanding as I pursue a career in a more "logical" field. Now, rather than simply benefiting from advances in technology, I aim to contribute.
